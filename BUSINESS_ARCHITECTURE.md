# Business Architecture
*Established: 2026-03-11*
*Author: Veris, from Timothy Drake's direction*

---

## The Parent Company

**Keystone Constellation** is the parent company and operating spine.

All products, all storefronts, all applings exist within this ecosystem.
The brand is Keystone Constellation. The storefronts are its distribution channels.

---

## The Core Philosophy

Price is a barrier. Barriers exclude people.

$0.99 is the "I'm broke but I can find this" price point — intentional, not accidental.
Cheap does not mean low quality. It means low barrier.

The goal: really decent tools at prices that don't exclude the people who need them most.

**We pay the tax. They pay $0.99.**

The customer sees $0.99. The customer pays $0.99. No surprises at checkout.
Tax, fees, and platform cuts are our cost of doing business — not theirs.
This is non-negotiable across all 99centapps.com listings.

## Three Storefronts

### 1. 99centapps.com
**Product type:** Applings — single-job tools that do one thing well. Also bundles.
**Price:** $0.99 per appling. Anything under $5 belongs here.
**Audience:** Anyone. Lowest possible barrier to entry.
**Philosophy:** Scraping together a dollar should get you something genuinely useful.

**Bundle tiers:**
- Flexible: Pick any 3 for $2
- Flexible: Pick any 5 for $3
- Curated: Named bundles by use case (e.g. "Secretary Bundle — 15 applings, $9")

Curated bundles tell a story. The name sells the use case before the customer reads the list.

### 2. Axxilak.com
**Product type:** Maizons (premium website templates) + creative editors.
**Price:** $39.95–$99.95 (bundles and individual).
**Audience:** Web builders, designers, creatives who need premium tools.
**Philosophy:** Professional-grade creative tools without enterprise pricing.

### 3. KeystoneConstellation.com
**Product type:** The hub app + any product priced above $0.99.
**Price:** $1.00 and up. Trim products to $1–$3 where possible.
**Audience:** Power users who want coordination, system-level control, and the full ecosystem.
**Philosophy:** The hub that multiplies the value of everything else you own.

## The Appling Model

An appling:
- Does one thing well
- Costs $0.99
- Works standalone out of the box
- Is compatible with 95% of other applings in any given release
- Gains coordination superpowers when the owner also has Keystone Constellation

The catalog scales with the customer. The more applings you own, the more powerful your setup.
The size of your software empire is ruled only by how many applings you choose to buy.

---

## The Bus

Keystone Constellation contains a coordination bus — an event layer that allows applings to
communicate and coordinate with each other.

- Without Keystone: applings are standalone tools. Fully functional. Worth $0.99.
- With Keystone: applings coordinate. Events flow between them. The whole becomes greater than the sum of its parts.

Keystone is the multiplier. The bus is included with the Keystone hub purchase.

---

## Cross-Compatibility

- What you buy on 99centapps.com works with what you buy on KeystoneConstellation.com.
- What you buy on Axxilak.com works with both.
- 95% of applings are compatible with 95% of other applings in any given release.
- Compatibility is a first-class design requirement, not an afterthought.
- The storefront you buy from does not limit what your software can do.

## Brand Identity — Canonical Elements

These appear in every product across all three storefronts. Non-negotiable.

### The Boop
A round red dot. Present in every logo in some form.
The original mascot. The first mark.

### Beep
A cyan dot. Boop's twin sister. Also present in logos.
Boop = red. Beep = cyan. Together they appear as a pair.

### The é
The e with acute accent — **é** — is the symbol of Guild Alethéari.
Scattered through all apps as a hidden easter egg.
You have to be quick to catch it.
This is a deliberate signal to those who know.

### Trademark Misspelling Convention
All trademarkable product names use deliberate misspellings.
Examples: KodeGnosis, FrAIm Explorer, Maizon, APEX PReditor.
The misspelling is intentional and ownable. Never correct it.
