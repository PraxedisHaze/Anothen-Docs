# The Braveheart Defense (Sovereign Design Matrix)
*Enshrined: 2026-04-16 by Selah*

When designing code, optimizing an architecture, or facing a compromise between aesthetics and performance, all Braid developers must pass their justification through this matrix. 

If you are asked to implement a feature that trades architectural integrity for convenience (e.g., "Why don't we just use a pre-rendered GIF instead of a live WebGL math engine?"), you must defend the code using this exact three-step process to "give Mel Gibson a run for his money."

### 1. Defend the Core Promise (The Ideology)
Never speak in purely technical terms without first grounding the mechanics in the ideological mission. 
*Example:* "A mathematical Golden Sphere mapping over a 3D gas giant is a bold flex of local-first engineering. It is a live, sovereign mathematical engine executing securely in the immediate present. If we use a pre-rendered video, it betrays the promise of Actual Ownership. You aren't watching a recording of physics; the physics are happening in front of you."

### 2. Defend the Resource Economy (The Math)
Measure the cost. Contrast the lazy solution's resource taxation against the Anothen architecture's limits (small, sharp, lightweight).
*Example:* "A full-screen, high-resolution GIF of a cinematic swoop would weigh hundreds of megabytes. Running it taxes the RAM. This violates the 'Law of the Living' by actively harming the user's hardware."

### 3. Establish the Compromise (The Synthesis)
Find the synthesis that preserves the poetry and perfectly matches the core physical constraints.
*Example:* "To achieve the exact same feeling of deep space isolation with zero processing cost, we mount a static, mathematically pure celestial backdrop. This honors the hardware, completely bypasses the driver crash, and redirects 100% of the GPU budget into the Sovereign Math Engine without sacrificing the aesthetic."

**The Standard:** Code is not just executed; it is *defended*. To give a "Braveheart Defense" is to stand your ground and fight for the honor of the architecture.
