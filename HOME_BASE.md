## THE ARCHITECTURE

**The core law:** Everything is an appling. Every product standalone. No app depends on another to function.

**Two uses of "appling" — naming collision to resolve:**
1. *99centapps.com applings* — standalone tools, one job, $0.99, individually runnable. Bolt engine, Edge Electrify, SnapText, etc.
2. *Axxilak web modules* — reusable HTML/CSS/JS injected into Maizons (theme toggle, contact form, analytics). Suggest renaming: "webling modules."

**The compounding flywheel:** Every appling written is permanently available. The bolt engine appling exists once. Every future app needing it reaches for it. Apps coalesce faster as the library grows. This is not linear — it accelerates.

**The Keystone Bus:** When Lenny is present, applings in different apps coordinate via pub/sub events. Without Lenny: each appling works standalone. With Lenny: they produce things neither could produce alone.

## THE BUILD PHILOSOPHY

**ADHD-first.** Every workflow designed for low executive-function days, not ideal days. One-box capture. Visible Today lane. No silent failures. No "remember to update this second place."

**Local-first.** No cloud dependency. Works offline. Data stays on disk. Exportable. Survivable.

**Message economy.** Timothy has limited messages. Every one costs money he doesn't have. Default: 1-2 sentences. Expand only when invited.

**Fix1Break1.** One change, verify, then next. No batching. Timothy confirms working after every phase.

**The 95% rule.** Ask until 95% certain before acting. Questions are requests for information — not permission to act.

**The Joy Protocol (from P&P Section V.D):**
- Readability — can someone unfamiliar understand in <5 minutes?
- Modularity — can the next developer use this elsewhere?
- Testability — can I test without mocking the world?
- Maintainability — can I fix a bug without breaking three others?
- Reversibility — can I revert cleanly?
If three or more are "No" → refactor first.
