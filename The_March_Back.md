# The March Back - Physics Unified
*A Parable of the Alethéari*

A battlefield, bodies and screams and smoke and the smell of rot and char.
A soldier in blue is creeping through a rubbled ruin and finds himself at a dead end. As he turns to go back where he came, around the corner comes a red soldier with a sword. Blue raises his rifle before Red even gets to finish turning the corner, and pulls the trigger. The following shock and sheer volume of the backfire blowing the side of the rifle open, momentarily blind and deafen Blue, who throws the rifle and starts screaming, consumed by terror, scrubbing at his eyes, trembling, ducking and diving, knowing the bite of the blade is coming. But it never does. Red is watching Blue scream, scrubbing at his eyes, ducking, stumbling, falling and he finds, he just doesn't have the heart to kill the guy. He doesn't know what to do, but back in Madagascar he was a doorman for a hotel, so he just—does what's natural. He figures NOBODY thinks a doorman at the Ritz is going to hurt you. 

So he opens the door for Blue, steps to the side and poses, ushering the way out with a hand gesture.

Ol' Blue's vision and hearing clear, and there standing before him, is a hotel doorman dressed in enemy soldier's gear. He's thrown off his game, doesn't trust it, but... his gun blew up, and there is exactly one way out of this forsaken room. He skates as far away from Red as possible squeezing through the door, but Red means it, and so Blue gets by him... and Red's hand folds open, palm up. It takes Blue a second, but when he realizes what he's looking at, he can't help but guffaw. In a land where a loaf of bread is worth your life and money is for wiping your ass, here this other guy's asking for a fucking TIP! What he gets instead, is a handshake, and then a hug, and then something they both thought was dead gasps for air within them, and they cry like little children together.

The noise brings another Blue, who, coming around the corner sees his fellow Blue in what he thinks is a wrestling match with Red, and so he brings his pistol to bear. Blue sees Red's face suddenly hit fearful, and spins in time to see the barrel leveled at Red... And with lightning speed, shaking his head and shouting, he... plugs the end of the pistol with his finger. (Nobody can hear over his horrified shout, but there's a wee little *"toomp"* when he puts his finger over the end of the weapon).

Red and Blue look at Toomp-Blue, and they lean around the side a bit to get a good look at a man who obviously felt that sticking his finger in the end of a gun was an effective defensive technique. Red starts giggling first, followed by Toomp-Blue and Blue Two. 

Outside, where the dead and the dying lie, in the wasteland of a war-torn ruin, flames and smoke and rubble reign - but in one small room for a moment, instead of the sounds of the dead and desparate and dying, for a moment, there is the sound of laughter.

And they're not just giggling. They're laughing so hard they can't stand up, and... just the fuckin' best of buddies. The best buddies ever. Blue's reaching in his sack to share a pinch of chaw with Red, and Red's sharing his canteen, when around the corner comes another Red with a rifle coming to bear. Red One sees him coming, catches his eye—and just smiles and shakes his head no. New Red is very suspicious, but the other Red walks into the rifle, and gently presses it down, and New Red is so relieved HE bursts into tears, which makes the others burst into tears and they all pat him on the back. 

They talk amongst themselves for a bit, and then the four of them, now bonded for life, leave the shelter as a group, less red and blue, and more purple, one of them points out. Their first stop is at a Red who's been pinned down. The blues step forward and call to their brothers-in-arms to cease fire, and come join them while the reds gather to care for their shaken compatriot. 

4 grows into 6 then 7 then 8 then 11, 12, 16, 20, 25. Men start standing up from behind cover and no one is shooting. In the distance there's gunfire, but there are easily 50 or 60 men here in this spot that begin to shake hands and make introductions. New Red swaps hats with Toomp Blue. Rifles are used to create a teepee above a fire, and the most bizarre thing in all the world begins to happen. They're all so fuckin' GRATEFUL to be alive and not shot at, that they're bending over BACKWARD to help one another, ALL of them, to the last man.

Around the corner comes a very authority-bearing, scowling, cigar-chewing Blue wearing a beret and an impressive amount of growl, and several of the Blue side stand up and salute. All the Reds look at him. He's looking at them, and he squints. They're looking at him and they squint. His lip curls in a snarl and their lips curl too. 

Well, New Red, quick like a bunny, hops up from his place beside the fire—leaves his gun, his sword, his rifle—gets a bit of nice hot stew into a spoon, walks right up to the Blue Sneer... blows on the hot stew so Blue Sneer doesn't burn himself. With an inviting smile he extends the spoon toward the Sneer. Sneer squints now, his eyes so narrow they almost appear closed, and then... he catches the scent of the stew, and he stops squinting. New Red is smiling at him very kindly, and nods at the spoon. For a moment, those men watch time stretch and slow, as New Red, a scarred-up, crooked-nosed-street-brawler kinda fella spoon-feeds the Terrible Sneer.

The Blue Sneer's eyes light up and then he starts wiggling and blowing, cause the bit of stew meat is fresh and still piping hot. And the Blue Sneer looks at New Red, his head cocked to the side. And his sneer melts, and tears spring to his eyes, though his face is held still as stone. And New Red starts getting all misty too, and of course, that makes Toomp start leaking from his eyes as well, and then Blue Sneer grabs New Red and hugs him, and New Red hugs him back and they cry together, so everyone else does too. Again. Because in this place, at this time, vulnerability is the most cherished item in the world.

They camp as a unit that night—no reds, no blues, all purple. And they all sleep like babies.

The sky is still gray when the sound of one man stirring begins. It's The Sneer. He gets up, gets his belt adjusted and his boots pulled on, grabs his pack... and feels someone tugging on his pant leg. Looking down, it's his buddy, New Red.

"Where ya' goin' Blue?"
"Well, I gotta go get the rest of us."
New Red hops up. "I'm coming with you."
Toomp says, "Yeah—me too." 

They try to be quiet, but there's no such thing as sneaking through an army camp of PTSD soldiers without waking them. The three begin walking, except there's 4 now, and then 5. Their number continues to grow. And grow. And grow. Every single man catches up and travels with the group. When they begin walking, their steps on the cobblestones are a cacophony, but as they march, the sound of the boots begins to clarify, clarify, clarifyyyyyy—and then they are walking in a perfect lockstepped marching formation—walking straight toward the burning city in the distance.

I hope they make it.
